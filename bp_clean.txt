Technical Blueprint: Architecture and Implementation of an Academic Integrity Analyzer The detection of stitched plagiarism— a sophisticated form of academic dishonesty where an author splices disparate paragraphs from multiple uncredited sources into a single document— represents a formidable challenge for traditional natural language processing systems. Conventional similarity checkers rely on exact string matching or localized n-gram overlaps. These systems catastrophically fail when confronted with intelligently paraphrased text, translated sources, or the seamless integration of highly specific, contextually distinct passages. To effectively counteract this, an "Academic Integrity Analyzer" must pivot away from lexical overlap and instead evaluate the structural, linguistic, and temporal metadata embedded within the text's inherent architecture. This technical blueprint details the comprehensive engineering architecture for a highly modular, deterministic system optimized for a 24-hour development cycle executed by a solo full-stack engineer. The architectural paradigm prioritizes "Functional Correctness"— encompassing absolute stability, deterministic execution, and the graceful handling of extreme edge cases. The system eschews the latency, rate limits, and non-determinism of external generative artificial intelligence in favor of robust open-source libraries, local dense vector embeddings, and rigorous unsupervised mathematical clustering. 1.
